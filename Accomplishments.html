Born

George Herman Ruth, Jr., was born on February 6, 1895, in Baltimore, Maryland. Part of the aura surrounding Ruth arose from his modest origins. Though the legend that he was an orphan is untrue, Ruth did have a difficult childhood.


Parents

Both his parents, George Herman Ruth, Sr., and Kate Shamberger Ruth, came from working-class German families. Ruth, Sr., owned and operated a saloon in a tough neighborhood on the Baltimore waterfront.


Past Life

In 1936 sportswriters honored Ruth by selecting him as one of five charter members to the newly established Baseball Hall of Fame in Cooperstown, New York. Ruth was a hopeless spendthrift, but, fortunately for him, in 1921 he met and employed Christy Walsh, a sports cartoonist-turned-agent.
