First

Ferrari's focus on Formula 1 cars led to much success; in 1953 and 1954 Ferrari's cars won the World Drivers Championship. Between 1960 and 1965, Ferraris won every Le Mans, and between 1958 and 1964 they won six of the seven Sebring races. His cars are still leaders in the performance world today.


First Car

1957–61 Ferrari 250 Testa Rossa by Britannica on Sketchfab

The first car to be designed and built entirely by his company under the Ferrari name—the 125 S—was not ready until 1947. It had a V-12 engine, which would become a signature feature of Ferrari’s cars, and it secured Ferrari’s first Grand Prix victory, in Rome in May 1947.


Finally

Following World War II, Ferrari race cars achieved more fame as they won several prestigious racing titles, including the 1948 race at Le Mans and the 1952 Grand Prix World Championship. From 1948-1988, Enzo Ferrari’s cars won over 5,000 races and earned 25 world titles.
